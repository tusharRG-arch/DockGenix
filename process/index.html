Our Process

The DockGenix Computational Engine

A structured, reproducible path from hypothesis to decision-grade outputs — engineered for scientific rigor and operational speed at every step.

1

Scientific Scoping

We align on the target context, constraints, available data, and success metrics before any computation begins. Clear scoping keeps the program focused on the decision you actually need to make.

  • Target context
  • Constraint mapping
  • Data audit
  • Success metrics
2

Mechanism Modeling

Docking, molecular dynamics, and interaction analysis evaluate feasible binding and surface failure modes early — so weak hypotheses are filtered before they consume wet lab budget.

  • Molecular docking
  • MD simulations
  • Interaction analysis
  • Failure-mode review
3

Quantitative Prioritization

Comparative ranking and quality filters focus resources on high-priority candidates. Confidence scoring and liability screening make the shortlist defensible in technical review.

  • Comparative ranking
  • ADMET-oriented filters
  • Liability screening
  • Confidence scoring
4

Decision Dossier

You receive clear reports, reproducible method settings, and recommended next experiments — packaged for your technical and leadership teams to act on immediately.

  • Reproducible settings
  • Ranked options
  • Next experiments
  • Review-ready reports

The Output

From Prediction to Proof

Every engagement is designed to end with something your team can defend and act on — not a black-box score. Method settings, assumptions, and run context travel with the results so findings can be reviewed, reproduced, and extended.

Where a program calls for it, computational shortlists flow directly into wet lab validation, closing the loop between prediction and experimental evidence. The result is decision-grade guidance suited to technical reviews, PI discussions, regulatory submissions, and R&D prioritization.

Timelines are matched to your objective through one of our engagement models — from a rapid Feasibility Sprint to long-form Collaborative Research.
