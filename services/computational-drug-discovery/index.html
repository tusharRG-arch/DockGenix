Capability 01

Computational drug discovery

Structured screening and prioritization workflows that support a defined molecular research decision.

Problems addressed

Reduce a broad candidate question to reviewable priorities

A computational workflow can help compare hypotheses, expose weak assumptions, and focus experimental resources. It does not independently prove efficacy, safety, or biological activity.

Suitable stages

Early target assessment, virtual screening strategy, hit comparison, and hypothesis refinement.

Typical audiences

Drug-discovery teams, biotechnology researchers, medicinal chemistry collaborators, and academic groups.

Methods considered during scoping

Structure reviewVirtual screeningDockingComparative scoringProperty filtersInteraction analysis

Potential deliverables

  • Documented screening or comparison workflow
  • Prioritized candidate set with stated criteria
  • Interaction and risk-focused interpretation
  • Assumptions, limitations, and next experiments

Limitations

Computational ranking is model- and input-dependent. It does not replace experimental confirmation, clinical evidence, or regulatory assessment.

Inputs to discuss
  • Target and decision context
  • Available structures or sequences
  • Candidate library and provenance
  • Known comparators or controls
  • Desired timeline and output
