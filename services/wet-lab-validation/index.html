Capability 04

Wet-lab validation

Project-scoped experimental follow-up designed to test a defined biological or computational hypothesis.

Problems addressed

Translate a prediction into a testable experimental question

Applicable methods, samples, facilities, biosafety requirements, responsibilities, and access arrangements must be confirmed before an experimental scope is accepted.

Suitable stages

Hypothesis testing, assay planning, enzyme studies, microbial or biochemical follow-up, and validation-oriented collaboration.

Typical audiences

Computational teams needing experimental follow-up and research groups requiring connected analysis.

Experimental areas to evaluate during scoping

Assay designMicrobiologyBiochemistryEnzyme studiesFermentationBioactive testing

Quality controls to define

  • Method suitability and controls
  • Sample identity, handling, and acceptance
  • Facility and equipment access
  • Replicates, deviations, and reporting rules

Access statement

This page does not claim that every listed method, instrument, or facility is owned or continuously available in-house. The delivery and access model is confirmed per project.

Inputs to discuss
  • Hypothesis and expected signal
  • Sample or material type
  • Required controls
  • Biosafety and handling needs
  • Timeline and decision threshold
