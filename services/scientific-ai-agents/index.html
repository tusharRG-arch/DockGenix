Capability 06

Scientific AI & agents

AI-supported research workflows designed around bounded tasks, reviewable sources, evaluation criteria, and human oversight.

Problems addressed

Use AI to support a defined research task without hiding uncertainty

A scientific AI system should be evaluated against its users, data, source material, failure modes, and the consequence of an incorrect output.

Suitable stages

Knowledge exploration, bounded retrieval, analysis support, screening assistance, and domain-focused interface design.

Typical audiences

Research teams with a defined workflow, source corpus, review process, and evaluation need.

Methods considered during scoping

RetrievalScientific searchClassificationPrioritizationAgent workflowsEvaluation design

Required controls

  • Data and source provenance
  • Task-specific evaluation set
  • Citation and grounding checks
  • Privacy, retention, and access controls

Limitations

AI outputs can be incomplete, biased, unsupported, or incorrect. Public scientific agents are not presented as clinical, diagnostic, regulatory, or autonomous research authority.

Inputs to discuss
  • Users and task boundary
  • Approved knowledge sources
  • Expected answer format
  • Evaluation criteria
  • Privacy and deployment needs
