Capability 03

Bioinformatics & multi-omics

Reproducible biological data workflows designed around an explicit interpretation or prioritization question.

Problems addressed

Turn complex biological data into a transparent analysis path

The workflow should begin with study design, data quality, metadata, and the intended inference rather than a fixed list of plots.

Suitable stages

Exploratory analysis, comparative studies, pathway interpretation, target prioritization, and pipeline design.

Typical audiences

Biotechnology teams, academic laboratories, translational researchers, and collaborative programs.

Methods considered during scoping

Data QCGenomicsProteomicsPathway analysisDifferential analysisData integration

Quality controls to define

  • Metadata and study-design review
  • Preprocessing and exclusion rules
  • Reference databases and versions
  • Multiple-testing and interpretation limits

Limitations

Associations do not automatically establish mechanism or causality. Batch effects, sample size, study design, database coverage, and preprocessing choices affect interpretation.

Inputs to discuss
  • Study question and design
  • Data type, format, and volume
  • Metadata and group definitions
  • Prior processing steps
  • Required output and audience
