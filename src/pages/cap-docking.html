Capability 02

Molecular docking & dynamics

Interaction hypotheses and comparative molecular behavior interpreted with transparent preparation, parameters, and limitations.

Problems addressed

Examine plausible interactions without mistaking a score for proof

Docking and molecular dynamics can help assess poses, interaction patterns, stability hypotheses, and comparative behavior when the structural inputs and question are suitable.

Suitable stages

Binding-hypothesis exploration, candidate comparison, mutation assessment, and simulation-informed follow-up.

Typical audiences

Computational biology, medicinal chemistry, structural biology, and academic research teams.

Methods considered during scoping

Structure preparationDocking protocolPose analysisMolecular dynamicsRMSD/RMSFInteraction persistence

Quality controls to define

  • Structure quality and preparation decisions
  • Protocol controls and comparative references
  • Replicate or convergence strategy where appropriate
  • Software versions, parameters, and analysis windows

Limitations

Docking scores are not binding affinities, and simulation stability is not biological efficacy. Interpretation depends on force fields, sampling, preparation, and system assumptions.

Inputs to discuss
  • Target structure or sequence
  • Ligands or variants
  • Known binding context
  • Comparators and constraints
  • Decision the analysis supports
